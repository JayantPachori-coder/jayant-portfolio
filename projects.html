i-mads project

I recently completed the i-MADS project, which stands for Image Processing using Math & Data Structures. This 6-8 week project is designed for first-year students enrolled in courses such as Linear Algebra & Differential Equations and Data Structures & Algorithms. In this integrated project, I explored the application of key linear algebra and data structures concepts in image processing, using industry-standard tools like LabelBox. The project provided hands-on exposure to real-life industry work scenarios, bridging the gap between theoretical knowledge and practical applications In addition to being a participant, I also served as a Learning Manager, coordinating activities and facilitating communication between first-year participants, second-year mentors, and industry professionals. This role enhanced my leadership and organizational skills, as I ensured smooth collaboration and effective learning throughout the project. The project was mentored by second-year students who had previously completed it, offering valuable guidance and insights. Additionally, we had the opportunity to interact with industry professionals, gaining firsthand knowledge and understanding of industry practices. This experience was an excellent opportunity to apply my coursework in a practical setting and develop skills relevant to the industry.
